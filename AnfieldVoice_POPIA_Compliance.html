About this document. This POPIA Compliance Statement outlines how Red Cape Technologies (Pty) Ltd processes personal information on behalf of residential estates using the AnfieldVoice platform. It serves as the Data Processing Addendum between Red Cape (as operator) and the Client estate or body corporate (as the responsible party). This document should be read together with the AnfieldVoice Terms of Service and the Client's own privacy policy.

2. Roles & Responsibilities

Responsible Party: The Client (Estate / Body Corporate)

Determines which personal information is collected, for what purpose, and how it is used. Responsible for obtaining consent from residents, providing privacy notices, and responding to data subject requests.

Operator: Red Cape Technologies (Pty) Ltd

Processes personal information only on documented instructions from the Client. Implements security measures, maintains records, assists with compliance, and ensures lawful processing.

This relationship is governed by Section 21 of POPIA, which requires that an operator process information only with the knowledge and authority of the responsible party, and maintain the security of the information.

4. Lawful Basis for Processing

AnfieldVoice processes Personal Information on the following lawful bases under POPIA:

BasisApplication
ConsentResidents consent to the collection and use of their personal information when they activate their account, opt into the resident directory, and create visitor tickets. Visitors implicitly consent by using the visitor terminal. Consent can be withdrawn at any time by contacting the Client or Red Cape.
Performance of a ContractProcessing necessary to deliver the AnfieldVoice service to the Client, including resident account management, ticketing, and security verification.
Legal ObligationAudit trail retention for security incident investigation, compliance with POPIA record-keeping requirements, and response to lawful requests from the Information Regulator or law enforcement.
Legitimate InterestSecurity of the estate and its residents — processing visitor information, maintaining audit logs, and managing access decisions are in the legitimate interest of all parties.
Important: The Client (as responsible party) is required to provide residents with a privacy notice that explains these processing activities and their rights under POPIA. Red Cape can assist in drafting this notice.

5. Technical & Organisational Security Measures

Red Cape implements the following security measures to protect Personal Information against loss, unlawful access, and unauthorised processing, as required by Section 19 of POPIA:

Technical Measures

MeasureImplementation
Encryption in TransitTLS 1.3 enforced for all API, SIP signalling, WebSocket, and web traffic. No unencrypted communication accepted.
Encryption at RestAES-256 encryption for all stored data, including resident records, visitor information, and audit logs.
PIN ProtectionVisitor PINs are one-way hashed using bcrypt with a work factor of 12. Plaintext PINs are never stored. Valid PINs exist only in memory during validation.
Access ControlsRole-based access control with four distinct roles (Resident, Property Admin, Body Corporate, Security Officer). Fine-grained permissions per function.
AuthenticationStrong password policies. Session management with automatic timeout. Multi-factor authentication available for administrator accounts.
Database SecurityIP whitelisting, certificate-based authentication, separate read-only replicas for reporting.
Audit LoggingAll administrative actions, security decisions, and data access events are logged immutably. Logs cannot be modified or deleted.
Network SecurityFirewalled infrastructure, intrusion detection, DDoS protection, regular vulnerability scanning.
Backup & RecoveryHourly incremental backups, daily full backups, encrypted off-site storage. Recovery tested quarterly.
Software SecurityRegular automated security updates. Dependency vulnerability scanning. Code review process for all changes.

Organisational Measures

  • Access to production systems is restricted to authorised Red Cape personnel on a least-privilege basis
  • All personnel with access to Personal Information undergo POPIA awareness training
  • Confidentiality agreements in place for all employees and contractors
  • Data breach response plan in place — notification to Client and Information Regulator within 72 hours
  • Data Protection Officer (DPO) appointed and registered
  • Quarterly security reviews and annual penetration testing
  • Vendor due diligence for any subcontractors with data access

6. Data Subject Rights

POPIA grants Data Subjects the following rights. The Client (as responsible party) is primarily responsible for responding to these requests, with Red Cape providing reasonable assistance:

RightHow It Is Exercised
Right to AccessData Subjects may request confirmation of whether their Personal Information is processed, and request a copy of the information. Requests should be directed to the Client in the first instance. Red Cape will provide data exports within 14 days.
Right to CorrectionData Subjects may request correction of inaccurate or outdated information. Residents can update their own profile through the mobile app. Other corrections can be made through the administration portal or by contacting Red Cape.
Right to DeletionData Subjects may request deletion of their Personal Information. Red Cape will process deletion requests within 14 days, subject to legal retention requirements (audit logs may be retained for the period required by law).
Right to ObjectData Subjects may object to the processing of their Personal Information on reasonable grounds. The Client will assess the objection and respond within 14 days.
Right to Withdraw ConsentWhere processing is based on consent, the Data Subject may withdraw consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
Right to ComplainData Subjects have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator (Regulator) if they believe their rights under POPIA have been infringed. Contact details are provided in Section 11.

8. Cross-Border Data Transfer

8.1 The AnfieldVoice platform processes and stores all data within the Republic of South Africa. For cloud-hosted deployments, the hosting infrastructure is located in Amazon Web Services' Cape Town region (af-south-1).

8.2 No Personal Information is transferred outside of South Africa unless:

  • The Client provides explicit written consent; and
  • Appropriate safeguards are in place (binding corporate rules, standard contractual clauses, or an adequacy determination by the Information Regulator); and
  • The Data Subject has been informed of the transfer.

8.3 Push notifications to Apple (APNs) and Google (FCM) devices involve the transmission of a device token to servers located in the US. These tokens are non-identifying and do not contain Personal Information. The notification payload is encrypted end-to-end.

9. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach involving Personal Information processed through the AnfieldVoice platform, Red Cape shall:

  • Notify the Client's designated contact within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach
  • Provide details of the nature, extent, and possible impact of the breach
  • Identify the categories of Personal Information compromised (where known)
  • Describe the measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate potential harm
  • Assist the Client in notifying the Information Regulator and affected Data Subjects as required by Section 22 of POPIA
  • Cooperate fully with any investigation by the Information Regulator

Each breach will be documented, investigated, and remediated. A post-incident report will be provided to the Client within 14 days, including root cause analysis and preventive measures implemented.

10. Client Obligations

As the responsible party under POPIA, the Client agrees to:

  • Provide Privacy Notices: Inform residents and visitors about what Personal Information is collected, how it is processed, and their rights under POPIA
  • Obtain Consents: Obtain and manage resident consent for the processing of their Personal Information, including directory opt-in/opt-out preferences
  • Respond to Data Subjects: Handle data subject access requests, correction requests, and complaints within the timeframes required by POPIA
  • Maintain Accuracy: Ensure that resident account information is kept current and accurate
  • Access Control: Properly manage user accounts and permissions, deactivating accounts promptly when users no longer require access
  • Notify Red Cape: Inform Red Cape of any changes to lawful processing instructions or data handling requirements
  • Data Retention: Configure appropriate data retention periods in the administration portal to comply with estate policies and legal requirements
