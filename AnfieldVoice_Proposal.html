Security

Six layers of verification. One fail-safe design.

No single point of failure. No shortcut to entry. Every layer must be satisfied before anyone enters the estate.

1

Resident Authorisation

Every visit begins with an explicit resident action — creating a ticket and generating a PIN. No PIN, no entry. No exceptions.

2

Time-Bound PIN

Each PIN is valid for today only. Expired PINs are rejected automatically. A PIN from yesterday cannot be reused. Residents set the validity window (default: 24 hours).

3

Ticket Retrieval

The PIN retrieves exactly one ticket. If the ticket was revoked or doesn't exist, access is denied. No generic "this PIN is valid" — it's tied to a specific visitor, for a specific apartment, on a specific day.

4

Security Officer Verification

The officer sees the full ticket — visitor name, vehicle, category, resident who authorised it — and visually confirms the person matches. The officer's identity is recorded on every admission.

5

Exception Handling

If anything doesn't match (wrong vehicle, different person, unexpected time), the officer calls the resident directly from the dashboard with one click. No admission without resolution.

6

Complete Audit Trail

Every decision is logged immutably: who created the ticket, when the PIN was used, which officer processed the visitor, what decision was made, and any exceptions. This data is searchable and never expired.

The design principle

Key Rule

A Non-Resident Property Administrator must never appear as an occupant

A letting agent who manages 20 apartments should never receive gate calls or generate visitor PINs for those units. They manage the tenancy — they don't live there.

Key Rule

Roles are additive, not exclusive

A resident who also owns their unit can be both Resident and Property Administrator. A security officer can also be Maintenance. No forced choices.
